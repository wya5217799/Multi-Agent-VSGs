## 3. R1 verdict — PASS (HEALTHY)

### 3.1 Verdict mechanics

```
diff = no_rf.r_f - baseline.r_f
     = -91.064 - (-84.162)
     = -6.901   sys-pu²

PASS condition: diff < -IMPROVE_TOL_R1 = -0.5
            -6.901 < -0.5  ⇒ PASS
```

`improvement_baseline_minus_no_rf = +6.901` (snapshot field).

### 3.2 Spurious-vs-Healthy check (V1 manual; per `_compute_r1_verdict` docstring)

R1 PASS could be **spurious** if `no_rf ≈ zero_all` (φ_f=0 train didn't learn
anything, just degenerate-to-no-control). Computed:

```
|no_rf - zero_all|  =  |-91.064 - (-106.045)|  =  14.982
|zero_all|          =   106.045
relative gap        =  14.982 / 106.045  =  14.1%
```

Healthy threshold (per docstring) = relative gap > 5%. **14.1% ≫ 5%** ⇒
**HEALTHY**.

`no_rf` is positioned between `baseline` and `zero_all`, leaning closer to
`baseline` (87% of the way from zero_all to baseline by r_f). This means
`no_rf` did learn meaningful r_h/r_d control during 200 ep, just lost the
`r_f` signal. Exactly the design intent of R1.

### 3.3 Interpretation

**φ_f penalty IS the causal driver of the trained-policy r_f improvement.**

Removing φ_f from training (KUNDUR_PHI_F=0) preserves ~69% of the total
improvement (14.98 / 21.88) — that's r_h/r_d acting through their own
penalties. The remaining ~31% (6.90 / 21.88) is attributable to φ_f
penalty's gradient signal training the policy to coordinate frequency
control across agents.

This is consistent with paper §IV-C reward formulation: `r_f` carries the
multi-agent frequency-coordination cost; `r_h` / `r_d` carry per-agent
parameter-deviation costs. Both layers contribute, both layers were
designed to.

## 5. Wall-time calibration vs Plan §10

Plan §10 estimated 200-ep `--phase-c-mode full` short-train at **10-50 hr**.
Actual:

| stage | plan estimate | actual | variance |
|---|---|---|---|
| Phase 5 (5 ep × 6 paper_eval) | ~10-15 min | **374.8 s = 6.2 min** | -50% |
| Phase 6 short-train (200 ep φ_f=0) | **10-50 hr** | **2402.8 s = 40 min** | **-95% to -98%** |
| Phase 6 eval (5 ep) | ~30-60 s | 62.2 s | OK |
| **total** | 10-50 hr | **2839.8 s = 47 min** | **-98%** |

**Root cause**: plan §10 estimated wall using **production train** speed
(scenario_set=test manifest cycle, update_repeat=10, full per-step monitor
overhead). Phase C short-train uses defaults (`scenario_set=none`, random
per-episode, `update_repeat` from config) that turn out ~50× faster per ep:

- Production train (per `kundur_simulink_20260430_*` runs): ~3-5 min/ep
- Phase C short-train (this run): **~12 s/ep** (verified at ep50/100/150
  ckpt mtime gaps of 597-598 s each).

**Action**: update `Plan §10` (next §6 to-do) to reflect 12 s/ep × 200 = 40
min realistic budget for full mode. This makes `--phase-c-mode full`
practical for routine use; the 10-50 hr ceiling estimate was protecting
against a non-existent scenario.
